# Slide Copy Patterns

Reference examples extracted from Julian's decks. These patterns define what GOOD slide copy looks like and what to avoid. Read this file during Phase 1 (Content Structuring) to calibrate extraction quality.

Source decks: C&T Interview Presentation (01.14.25), Highsnobiety Innovation Phase 2, Moet Hennessy New Rules Event Deck.

---

## Pattern 1: Headlines ARE the Slide

Most slides have zero body copy. The headline carries the full idea by itself. If a headline needs body copy to make sense, the headline is too weak.

**Good (from C&T deck):**
- "AMID WIDESPREAD DATING APP FATIGUE, BUMBLE IS FARING WORST."
- "KNOWN AS A RELIC OF THE BYGONE 2015 GIRL-BOSS ERA, BUMBLE IS SEEN AS OUT-OF-TOUCH, PROBLEMATIC AND CRINGE."
- "NO MATTER THE BRIEF, I PRACTICE STRATEGY AS A CREATIVE RELATIONSHIP TO THE UNKNOWN"

**Bad (what AI tends to produce):**
- Headline: "THE CHALLENGE" / Body: "Bumble is struggling with dating app fatigue and a perception of being outdated."
- Headline: "OUR APPROACH" / Body: "We practice strategy as a creative relationship to the unknown."

The difference: Julian's headlines ARE the argument. AI headlines are labels that require body copy to explain.

## Pattern 2: Eyebrows Are Labels, Headlines Are Arguments

Eyebrows (serif italic, small, structural) tell you WHAT KIND of idea this is. Headlines tell you THE IDEA.

**Good:**
- Eyebrow: *The Challenge* / Headline: "AMID WIDESPREAD DATING APP FATIGUE, BUMBLE IS FARING WORST."
- Eyebrow: *Cultural Truth* / Headline: "ROMANCE HAS ALWAYS BEEN SO MUCH BIGGER THAN JUST DATING OR EVEN RELATIONSHIPS."
- Eyebrow: *Human Insight* / Headline: "BEING A ROMANTIC IS THE ORIGINAL HUMAN SUPERPOWER"

**Bad:**
- Eyebrow: *The Challenge* / Headline: "BUMBLE'S BRAND PERCEPTION PROBLEM"
- Eyebrow: *Cultural Truth* / Headline: "THE STATE OF MODERN ROMANCE"

The bad versions are labels-on-labels. The good versions make the eyebrow redundant — you could read the headline alone and get the full idea. The eyebrow adds navigational structure, not meaning.

## Pattern 3: Split Slides Create Tension Between Two Ideas

When a slide has two columns, each column carries an EQUAL but contrasting idea. They are NOT "summary left, evidence right."

**Good (from C&T deck):**
- Left (*Brand DNA*): "BUMBLE WAS CREATED TO EMPOWER WOMEN — THROUGH A DATING APP BUILT AROUND THEIR NEEDS"
- Right (*Cultural Truth*): "BUT A DECADE LATER, WOMEN'S ROMANTIC EXPERIENCE IS MORE FRAUGHT THAN EVER BEFORE."

Both sides are headlines. Both are complete thoughts. The tension between them is the point.

**Bad (what the Imitation Premium v2 deck did):**
- Left (bold summary): "Commissioner Adam Silver called it 'snacks versus meals'..."
- Right (regular evidence): "Then the product decisions served the open model..."

This is summary + continuation, not tension + contrast.

## Pattern 4: Color Highlights the Operative Word

Color is used on 1-2 words per slide to mark the word that changes the meaning. Not for decoration.

**Good:**
- "BUMBLE WAS CREATED TO **EMPOWER WOMEN**" — "EMPOWER WOMEN" in red. That's the operative claim.
- "A 360% LIFESTYLE **SERVICE** FOR A NEW GENERATION OF **MODERN** WOMEN." — "SERVICE" and "MODERN" underlined. Those are the two words that reframe Bumble.
- "IT **UNLOCKS CREATIVITY** BY INVITING US TO FIND **FANTASY IN THE MUNDANE**" — red on the payoff phrases.

**Bad:**
- Bolding every proper noun or statistic
- Highlighting whole sentences
- Using color for visual variety rather than semantic emphasis

## Pattern 5: Evidence Is Visual, Not Described

When you need to prove a cultural point, show the artifact. Don't describe it in body copy.

**Good:**
- Full-screen tweet screenshot (Bobby Mars: "Dating apps are the romantic equivalent of DoorDash..." — 577K views, 17K likes)
- Full-bleed photo with text overlay for the insight moment

**Bad:**
- Body copy that says: "As one viral tweet put it, dating apps feel spiritually broken."
- A bullet point listing social media statistics

The artifact IS the slide. The audience reads it themselves.

## Pattern 6: Repetition as Rhythm (The Mantra Slide)

Three parallel lines, same size, generous vertical spacing. No hierarchy between them. The repetition creates a mantra.

**Good (from C&T deck):**
```
IT TURNS THE WALK HOME INTO A RUNWAY

UNCERTAINTY INTO ADVENTURE

YOUR DAILY CHORES INTO BELOVED RITUALS
```

Each line follows the same structure (X INTO Y). Same font, same size, same weight. The rhythm does the work.

---

## Pattern 7: Progressive Reveal Builds

When introducing a framework with 2-3 parts, build it incrementally across slides rather than showing all parts at once.

**Good (from C&T deck):**
- Slide 7: One card (INTERROGATING)
- Slide 8: Two cards (INTERROGATING + DISTILLING)
- Slide 9: Three cards (INTERROGATING + DISTILLING + EXPRESSING)

The audience watches the model assemble. Each step lands before the next arrives.

---

## Pattern 8: From/To as Structural Device

When showing a transformation, use explicit "From" and "To" labels with corresponding word pairs.

**Good:**
```
From                          To
A BRAND PEOPLE RECOGNIZE      A BRAND PEOPLE LIVE BY

PRODUCT                       EXPERIENCE
BRAND                         CURATOR
VISIBILITY                    IMPACT
BASICS                        LIFESTYLE
```

The left column is the current state. The right column is the aspiration. A visual bridge or arrow connects them.

## Pattern 9: Copy Has Personality

Julian's slide copy uses distinctive, opinionated language. Not neutral summaries.

**Good:**
- "My constant curiosity has given me KALEIDOSCOPIC strategy credentials"
- "An ENDLESS, INFURIATING and multidisciplinary dance of:"
- "A creative relationship to THE UNKNOWN"
- "What should be a pathway to the MAGIC SIDE OF BEING HUMAN has become a CHORE"

**Bad (AI neutral equivalent):**
- "My diverse background spans multiple strategy disciplines"
- "A complex, multifaceted process involving:"
- "An approach that embraces ambiguity"
- "The dating experience has become transactional for many users"

The good versions use unexpected adjective choices (kaleidoscopic, infuriating, beloved), physical metaphors (runway, dance, chore), and emotional stakes (magic side of being human). The bad versions describe the same ideas without any texture.

---

## Anti-Pattern: The Two-Column Summary

The most common AI slide failure: left column is a bold summary of the section, right column is regular-weight evidence. Both columns say essentially the same thing at different levels of detail.

This happens because the AI tries to "fill" the two-column layout rather than putting two DIFFERENT ideas in tension. If both columns make the same point, one of them is redundant.

**The test:** Cover the left column. Does the right column make sense on its own? Cover the right column. Does the left make sense on its own? If both pass, they might be a real tension pair. If the right column only makes sense as an expansion of the left, you've written a summary + evidence layout. Restructure.
